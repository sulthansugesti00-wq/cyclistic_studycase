Modelling data

Creating a model to indicate whether a row is considered as “Commuter” or not

What is a commuter

Based on assumption commuter meet these criteria - Bike ride duration less than 30 minutes. - Morning commute window between 6 AM - 8 AM. - Evening commute window between 3 PM - 6 PM (Based on casual peak data). - Weekday only

Creating the model

model_data <- analysis_data %>% 
  mutate(day_of_week=wday(started_at),
         hour_of_day=hour(started_at), 
         ride_time=as.numeric(difftime(ended_at, started_at, units = "mins")),                 commuter=if_else(day_of_week %in% 2:6 & (hour_of_day %in% 6:8 | hour_of_day %in% 15:18)&ride_time <30, TRUE, FALSE))

This model flags rider that meet commuter criteria. Used for further analysis

Key findings & Recommendation

Key findings

  1. Around 32 percent of 2025 transactions are commuter based on model with assumption
  2. Cannot identify rider bike type preference due to electric bike consist of 60% of the whole fleet.
  3. 3 Highest average ride with casual proportion are Friday, Saturday, and Sunday with Saturday having the most casual proportion.
  4. 3 highest commuter station are, Clinton St & Washington Blvd, Kingsbury St & Kinzie St, Canal St& Madison St
  5. Peak hour for commuters are 4 PM - 6 PM, with 5PM having the highest casual proportion than the rest

Recommendation

This analysis recommends a two pronged digital marketing strategy

First, Weekday commuter targeting. Among peak hour riders, 350,000 casual riders exhibit member behavior that needs to commute. Targeting these riders at top station during 15:00 - 18:00 on weekdays with messages to converse casual into member would be a high quality conversion opportunity

Second, weekend awareness campaign. Weekend traffic shows the highest casual proportion. Broader awareness campaign during weekends will maximize casual rider exposure to membership promotions.

Should a combined conversion rates of 10% be achieved across both strategy, this translates to 1,144 new annual membership.
